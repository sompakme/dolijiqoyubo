Jason Cerbone (born November 2, 1977) is an American actor who has been in a number of films. He received his first acting role when he was four years old, in a commercial for Sesame Street. At the age of seven, he signed with the Ford Modeling Agency in New York City. Cerbone went on to feature in the music video for Bon Jovi’s “Silent Night,” as well as the main character in Suzanne Vega’s video for the song “Luka,” among other roles. After graduating from Sacred Heart High School in New York, he went on to Concordia College, where he earned a bachelor’s degree in biology. After graduating from college, he returned to the stage to continue his performing career. His most well-known part is that of Jackie Aprile, Jr. in the popular HBO series The Sopranos, in which he played the title character. Aside from that, Cerbone had a brief cameo role in the film Cloverfield, in which he appeared as a New York police detective. At The Players’ Theater in Greenwich Village’s Shortened Attention Span Theater Festival that year, Cerbone co-starred alongside Gina Ferranti and Ernest Mingione in Charles Messina’s play Merging, which won Best Play and Best Actress for Cerbone. Cerbone also appeared in the film adaptation of the novel Merging, which was published in 2009.
