Early Life and Basketball Career

Kenny Smith was born in Queens, New York, and his passion for basketball was evident from a young age. He played for Archbishop Molloy High School before attending the University of North Carolina. Smith’s professional career spanned over a decade, during which he played for several NBA teams, including the Houston Rockets, Detroit Pistons, Orlando Magic, and Denver Nuggets. His most notable achievements came with the Rockets, where he won two NBA Championships in 1994 and 1995.

Transition to Broadcasting

After retiring from professional basketball in 1997, Smith transitioned into broadcasting. He joined Turner Sports and has since become a prominent figure in sports analysis and commentary. His insightful analysis and charismatic personality have made him a fan favorite.
