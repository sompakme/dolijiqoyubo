Early Life and Career Beginnings

Linda Perry was born on April 15, 1965, in Springfield, Massachusetts. She developed a passion for music at a young age and started playing guitar and singing in her early teens. Perry moved to San Francisco in 1986 and began her music career as the lead singer of the band 4 Non Blondes.

Success with 4 Non Blondes

The band’s debut album, “Bigger, Better, Faster, More!” was a commercial success, with the hit single “What’s Up?” reaching number one in several countries. However, Perry left the band in 1994 to pursue a solo career.

Solo Career and Songwriting Success

After leaving 4 Non Blondes, Perry released two solo albums, “In Flight” and “After Hours”. However, her biggest success came as a songwriter and producer for other artists. She has written and produced songs for numerous successful artists, including Christina Aguilera, Pink, Gwen Stefani, and Adele.

Work with Christina Aguilera

Establishment of Two Record Labels

Television and Film Contributions

Personal Life
