Who is Sarah Langs? A Profile of a Baseball Star and an ALS Fighter

Sarah Langs is a name that many baseball fans know and admire. She is a reporter and analyst for the MLB Network, who has been covering the sport with passion and insight for years. She is also a courageous woman who is battling ALS, a devastating disease that affects the nervous system. In this article, we will explore who Sarah Langs is, how she became a baseball star, and how she is coping with her condition.

Sarah Langs: A Baseball Lover Since Childhood

Sarah Langs was born in New York City and grew up in a family of baseball fans. Her father was a die-hard Yankees fan, while her mother was a Mets fan. Sarah inherited both of their loyalties and developed a love for the game at an early age. She attended her first game at Yankee Stadium when she was four years old, and soon became fascinated by the statistics and history of baseball.

Sarah Langs attended the University of Chicago, where she majored in history and minored in statistics. She also wrote for the student newspaper, The Chicago Maroon, covering sports and other topics. She graduated in 2012 with honors and landed an internship at the New York Daily News, where she worked as a sports reporter and editor.

A Rising Star in Baseball Media

Sarah Langs quickly made a name for herself in the baseball media industry, thanks to her impressive knowledge and enthusiasm. She joined ESPN in 2014 as a baseball researcher and media manager, working on various shows and platforms. She also became a regular guest on Buster Olney’s podcast, The Baseball Tonight Podcast, where she shared her insights and analysis on the latest trends and stories in baseball.

In 2021, Sarah Langs made history as part of the first-ever all-female broadcast team for MLB on ESPN. She joined play-by-play announcer Beth Mowins, analyst Jessica Mendoza, and reporter Marly Rivera for a game between the St. Louis Cardinals and the Miami Marlins on International Women’s Day. Sarah Langs described the experience as a “dream come true”, and received praise from fans and colleagues alike.

Sarah Langs joined the MLB Network in 2022 as a reporter and analyst, appearing on various shows such as MLB Now, MLB Tonight, The Rundown, and High Heat. She also continued to write for MLB.com, covering topics such as statistics, trivia, history, and awards. She became known for her catchphrase “Baseball is the best”, which reflected her genuine love and appreciation for the game.

Sarah Langs: A Brave Fighter Against ALS

In 2023, Sarah Langs received devastating news: she was diagnosed with ALS (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis), also known as Lou Gehrig’s disease. ALS is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that affects the nerve cells that control voluntary muscle movement. There is no cure for ALS, and most people with the disease die within three to five years of diagnosis.

Sarah Langs announced her diagnosis publicly on Twitter, saying that she was “shocked and heartbroken” by the news, but that she was “determined to fight this with everything I have”. She also thanked her family, friends, colleagues, and fans for their support and love.

Sarah Langs has since become an advocate for ALS awareness and research, using her platform to raise funds and educate people about the disease. She has partnered with Project ALS, a non-profit organization that funds scientific research to find effective treatments and cures for ALS. She has also launched a campaign called Stars for Sarah, which encourages people to donate to Project ALS and receive a star-shaped pin in return.

On June 2nd, 2023, Sarah Langs was honored by the MLB on Lou Gehrig Day, a day that commemorates the legendary Yankees’ first baseman who died of ALS in 1941. Sarah Langs attended the game between the New York Mets and the Toronto Blue Jays at Citi Field, where she received a warm welcome from players, coaches, staff, and fans. She wore a T-shirt that read “Baseball is who I am”, which was designed by Rotowear, a company that donated $57,000 to Project ALS from the sales of the shirt.
